    Influence of Ionospheric Disturbance on Radio Propagation Integrity and Communication Resilience

    Sensors, Energy, and Automation Laboratory (SEAL), University of Washington

    Aug 2025 – Present

    Role: Project manager, First Co-author

    Advisors: Prof. Alexander V. Mamishev, Prof. Sep Makhsous, Dr. Nick Donnangelo, Dr. Mickey Batson

    • Contribution Highlights:
      • Conducted database-driven HF SNR analysis by matching four Perseid meteor events (IMO Fireball Database) with thousands of amateur radio reception records from the Madrigal archive; developed a geometry-aware selection algorithm using great-circle paths and distance bins (0–500 km, 501–700 km, 701–900 km, etc.) to align propagation paths with meteor entry locations.
      • Applied event-locked short-time Fourier transform (STFT) to extract ΔSNR spectrograms around each meteor event, followed by baseline normalization and detrending to remove diurnal and seasonal variability.
      • The results demonstrated that individual meteor events can suppress or enhance HF link quality depending on path geometry, confirming that meteors can shift communication paths in or out of service.
    • Related Publications:
      • (First Co-authorship) X. Xu, Z. Liu, M. Yang, T. Agarwal, M. Mamishev, G. Nathan, S. Makhsous, N. Donnan- gelo, ”Influence of Meteor-Induced Ionospheric Disturbances on HF Radio Signal Propagation,” 2026 United States National Committee of URSI National Radio Science Meeting (USNC-URSI NRSM), Boulder, CO, USA, 2025, [Under Peer-review.]

    Quantitative Assessment of Soldering-Induced PM2.5 Exposure Using a Distributed Sensor Network

    Sensors, Energy, and Automation Laboratory (SEAL), University of Washington

    Oct 2024 – Mar 2025

    Role: Project participant, Co-author

    Advisors: Prof. Sep Makhsous, Prof. Alexander V. Mamishev, Mr. Gokul Nathan

    • Contribution Highlights:
      • Optimized and deployed AeroSpec, a low-cost IoT sensor network integrating PMSA003 optical particle counters, ESP32 microcontrollers, and E-Ink displays for high-resolution indoor air quality monitoring.
      • Implemented controlled soldering trials (15 sub-trials across 4 commercial purifiers and control condition) while synchronizing six AeroSpec sensors at breathing-zone and workstation-relevant positions.
      • Detected PM2.5 spikes up to 1400 μg/m3 ( 93× WHO 24-hr limit); found that all purifiers reduced exposure in the user’s breathing zone. Identified model-dependent efficiencies: HEPA-based overhead purifiers reduced PM2.5 by >95%, while low-cost carbon filter purifiers still mitigated exposure by redirecting particulates away from inhalation zones.
      • Developed evidence-based guidelines for air purifier selection and placement in instructional labs, offering action- able recommendations to reduce occupational exposure risks for 54,000+ U.S. electrical engineering students an- nually.
    • Related Publications:
      • I. M. Kinsella, A. N. Petrbokova, R. Yang, Z. Liu, G. Nathan, N. Thompson, A. V. Mamishev, and S. Makhsous, ”Quantitative Assessment of Soldering-Induced PM2.5 Exposure Using a Distributed Sensor Network in Instruc- tional Laboratory Settings,” Air, vol. 3, no. 2, p. 16, 2025.

    Sensor Fusion for Non-Intrusive Adaptive Distancing of Visually Impaired Users

    Sensors, Energy, and Automation Laboratory (SEAL), University of Washington

    Jan 2025 – Present

    Role: Embedded Engineer, First Co-author

    Advisors: Prof. Alexander V. Mamishev

    • Contribution Highlights:
      • Developed the FireFly prototype, a wearable Electronic Travel Aid (ETA) combining an AMG8833 thermal imaging array and VL53L1X Time-of-Flight LiDAR sensor for human recognition and obstacle detection.
      • Implemented I2C-based embedded integration on Arduino Nano with multimodal feedback (acoustic, vibration, LED) scaled to object type and proximity, enabling intuitive real-time alerts.
